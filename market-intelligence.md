If this build does one thing well, it must **make Placed Right the most trustworthy fence company in New Hampshire before a single phone call happens.** That means real project photos, transparent pricing ranges, a response-time guarantee, and content that proves deep NH expertise — frost line engineering, town-by-town permit guidance, pool code compliance. ## Section 4 — Pricing intelligence

### Cost per linear foot by material in New Hampshire

The NH fence market runs **10–30% above national averages** due to frost line depth requirements, rocky granite soil, and higher New England labor rates. These ranges reflect 6-foot residential fence, fully installed, based on cross-referenced data from Brentwood Fence (the only NH company publishing costs), ProMatcher NH-specific averages, HomeBlue Manchester data, and Angi regional estimates.

**Pressure-treated pine:** $15–$30 per linear foot installed (average $22/lf). The budget entry point. Materials alone run $4–$12/lf with labor adding $8–$13/lf.

**Cedar:** $25–$50 per linear foot installed (average $35/lf). The most popular premium wood choice in NH due to natural rot resistance in freeze-thaw conditions. Cedar board-on-board privacy fence sits at the high end.

**Vinyl/PVC:** $25–$60 per linear foot installed (average $40/lf). White vinyl is cheapest ($25–$35); wood-grain finishes run $35–$50; black vinyl commands premium pricing up to $60. Split-rail vinyl at $9–$12/lf provides a budget option for large properties.

**Aluminum/ornamental:** $25–$75 per linear foot installed (average $40/lf). Pool-code-compliant aluminum runs $30–$45/lf. Ornamental styles push $40–$75/lf. Coastal Seacoast installations require marine-grade powder coating, adding 10–15%.

**Chain link:** $10–$30 per linear foot installed (average $15/lf). Black vinyl-coated chain link at $10–$34/lf has largely replaced galvanized in residential applications.

### Typical total project ranges

For a **small yard (~100 linear feet)**: chain link $1,000–$2,500; pressure-treated wood $2,100–$3,500; cedar $2,500–$5,000; vinyl $3,000–$6,000. For an **average yard (~150–200 linear feet)**: most NH homeowners pay **$3,000–$10,000** depending on material — this is the core project range. For a **large yard (~300+ linear feet)**: projects typically run $6,000–$18,000+, with per-foot pricing dipping slightly as fixed costs spread across more footage.

### The psychological price ceiling

Buyer behavior shifts at distinct thresholds. Under **$3,000**, decisions happen quickly — often after a single quote. Between **$3,000 and $7,000** is the sweet spot where most NH residential projects land; buyers get 2–3 quotes but don't agonize. At **$7,000–$10,000**, sticker shock kicks in — buyers actively comparison shop, consider DIY or phased installation, and may delay. Above **$10,000**, this becomes a major home improvement decision requiring financing conversations. Multiple NH competitors (Finnegan, Fences Unlimited, Superior) prominently offer financing, confirming they regularly encounter projects in this range.

### How competitors handle pricing

**Nearly every NH fence company hides pricing entirely.** The universal model is "Free Estimate / Request a Quote." The lone exception is **Brentwood Fence**, which publishes extensive cost-per-foot ranges through blog content and dominates informational search results as a result. No NH competitor offers a project cost calculator or publishes sample project costs. Reliable Fence has a Virtual Fence Calculator, but it's a lead-gen tool, not a transparent price estimator.

### Pricing strategy recommendation for the website

Placed Right should position as **"Transparent Mid-Premium"** — not the cheapest, not the most expensive, but the first company that respects the buyer's intelligence with honest pricing. The website should publish **"starting at" ranges by material type** (e.g., "Wood privacy fences start at $25/linear foot installed") alongside a **"What's Included" breakdown** showing what the per-foot price covers: posts, rails, boards, concrete, hardware, labor, cleanup, DigSafe coordination, and permit assistance. Sample project galleries with price ranges ("This 150-foot cedar privacy fence with one walk gate: $5,200–$6,800") provide concrete anchoring.

Lead with per-foot pricing, not total project cost — "$35 per foot" feels manageable while "$5,250 for 150 feet" triggers sticker shock. Use **Good/Better/Best tiered packaging**: pressure-treated pine starting at $22/lf, cedar board-on-board starting at $32/lf, premium vinyl starting at $40/lf. Display financing prominently — a $7,000 fence at $150/month changes the purchase psychology entirely. And frame long-term value: wood requires staining every 2–3 years at $2–$14/lf per application, while vinyl costs virtually nothing to maintain over 20 years.

### NH-specific cost factors

**Frost line depth of 48 inches** (4 feet minimum, up to 60 inches in northern NH) requires 10-foot posts for 6-foot fences and significantly more concrete per hole. This adds approximately **$3–$8/lf** compared to regions with 24-inch frost lines. Rocky NH granite soil compounds costs — hydraulic auger or rock drilling equipment can add $200–$700+ to a project. Old fence removal runs $3–$7/lf. Property surveys ($200–$1,200) are strongly recommended. Seacoast projects run 5–15% above Southern NH due to labor transport and marine-grade material requirements. Seasonal pricing varies: **September–October is the sweet spot** for buyers — ground still workable, demand dropping, contractors may negotiate.

## Section 7 — Conversion patterns

### Above-the-fold requirements for mobile

Five elements must be visible without scrolling. **Company logo** in the top left, small enough to not dominate. **Click-to-call phone icon** in the top right — one tap to dial, always visible in a sticky header. A **primary headline** that is benefit-driven and location-specific: "NH's Family-Run Fence Experts" or "Beautiful Fences. Built Right. Built Here." A **primary CTA button** — full-width, high contrast, reading "Get Your Free Estimate." And a **hero image** showing a real completed fence project or lifestyle photograph with a family or pet in a fenced yard.

If space allows within the mobile viewport, include the tagline "Love Wins Where Our Pickets Begin," a secondary CTA ("Call 603-XXX-XXXX"), and a single-line trust micro-bar: "★★★★★ XX+ Google Reviews | NH Born & Family Run | Licensed & Insured." **Note the phone number:** The current (978) area code is a Massachusetts number, which directly undermines the "NH born and raised" positioning. Placed Right should acquire a 603 area code number for the website.

Avoid above the fold: sliding carousels (slow, low engagement), video backgrounds (kills mobile load time), full estimate forms (too much friction), large text blocks, badge overload, social media icons (sends people away), and stock photography.

### Friction points to eliminate

**Ghosting anxiety is the dominant friction point in this entire category.** Reviews across every NH fence company confirm it: contractors request deposits, schedule estimates, or promise callbacks — then vanish. The website must proactively address this with a **response-time guarantee** ("We respond within 4 hours or your first consultation is on us") and an automatic confirmation system that sends an immediate text or email when a form is submitted.

The current Placed Right contact form has only 3 fields (Name, Email, Message) with **no phone number field** — this is a critical failure. Phone is the primary contact method in home services. The form also lacks a fence type selector, address field, or project context, forcing the customer to write an unstructured paragraph. This increases cognitive load and reduces completions.

**Pricing opacity creates friction.** When buyers search "how much does a fence cost in NH" and every contractor says "call for a quote," they either call the one company that publishes ranges (currently Brentwood Fence) or they postpone the decision. Published price ranges reduce the fear of being ripped off and pre-qualify leads so the estimate call is more productive.

### Recommended form structure

Research consistently shows **4–6 fields in multi-step format** optimize conversion for service estimate forms. Forms with 5 or fewer fields achieve approximately 50% higher completion rates than 10+ field forms. For service businesses specifically, removing project-relevant fields (fence type, timeline) actually decreased conversions by 14% in one study — customers expect relevant context questions and feel more confident when the company asks them.

The recommended structure is three steps. **Step 1:** "What type of fence are you interested in?" — visual selector showing images of Wood, Vinyl, Chain Link, Aluminum, and "Not Sure" options. This is the low-commitment entry point that creates psychological investment. **Step 2:** "What's the main purpose?" — Privacy, Pool Safety, Pet/Child Safety, Decorative, or Property Line. **Step 3:** Name, Phone, Email, ZIP Code, and an optional "Anything else we should know?" textarea. This produces a pre-qualified lead with enough context for the estimator to prepare before the site visit.

Every homepage section should end with a CTA. Phone number should appear minimum 3 times on the homepage. A sticky mobile bottom bar with "Get Free Estimate" should persist through all scrolling.

## Section 8 — Design and aesthetic landscape

### What the NH fence market actually looks like

The NH fence contractor design landscape splits into two dominant palettes: **green-brown-white** (Finnegan, Stateline, Pinecrest — earthy, outdoor-coded) and **navy-white-gray** (Fences Unlimited, Granite State — corporate, safe). Typography is **universally sans-serif** across every single competitor — no NH fence company uses serif fonts anywhere. Photography ranges from excellent real job photos (Finnegan, Granite State) to catastrophically dated stock (Platinum Fence), with most companies landing somewhere in between with mixed real/stock imagery. No competitor uses before/after comparisons, drone/aerial photography, or lifestyle photography showing families actually using the fenced space.

Layout patterns are remarkably uniform: full-width hero image with text overlay, About section, services grid (3–4 cards), "Why Choose Us" icons, gallery thumbnails, testimonials, contact/footer. Most sites run on WordPress or Squarespace with template-level customization. Mobile experiences are universally adequate but none are truly mobile-first — no competitor has a genuinely excellent smartphone experience.

### Visual differentiation opportunity

The path to instant recognition for Placed Right Fence is **warmth in a cold industry.** Every competitor defaults to either cold corporate (navy/gray) or generic outdoor (green/brown). Placed Right's tagline ("Love Wins Where Our Pickets Begin") and family-run positioning demand a visual identity built on warmth and craft.

**Color direction:** Primary deep warm navy (#1B365D) for authority without coldness. Secondary soft terracotta/warm rust (#C4704B) — completely absent from the NH fence market, providing instant recognition. Accent warm cream/ivory (#F5F0E8) for an organic, handcrafted feel. CTA buttons in terracotta with white text for high-contrast, attention-grabbing calls to action. Subtle muted sage green (#7A8B6F) as a nod to the outdoor category without defaulting to the expected palette.

**Typography:** Heading font **DM Serif Display** (Google Fonts) — a warm, elegant serif that signals craft and premium quality. No NH competitor uses serif headings, making this an immediate differentiator. Body font **DM Sans** — clean, highly readable, pairs perfectly with DM Serif Display from the same type family. This pairing supports the tagline's poetic quality while maintaining excellent readability.

**Photography approach: "Life Behind the Fence."** Show what fences enable, not just the product itself. Wide shots of NH yards with families and pets enjoying the fenced space in golden-hour lighting. Seasonal photography leveraging NH's four distinct seasons — fences in fall foliage, winter snow, spring bloom, summer sun. Close-up craftsmanship shots of post-setting, leveling, and hardware details. Authentic team photos on the job. Drone/aerial views of completed properties showing the full transformation. **Design details:** Subtle textures (linen, wood grain), rounded corners, and soft shadows rather than sharp corporate edges. A custom illustrated picket fence motif as a recurring visual element — section dividers, icon style, footer decoration. Micro-interactions with gentle hover effects and scroll animations. The overall feel should be "approachable craftsman" — a website that feels handmade and personal, not templated and corporate.

## Section 9 — Strategic recommendations

### DO directives

**DO: Replace every stock photo with real project photography within 30 days of launch** — Placed Right's current site uses 100% Unsplash stock images identifiable by URL parameters. In this category, stock photos destroy credibility instantly. Competitors Finnegan and Granite State prove that authentic job photos are the baseline expectation. Photograph every project from multiple angles, include the yard context, and build a categorized gallery from day one.

**DO: Publish transparent pricing ranges by material and project size** — Every NH competitor except Brentwood Fence hides pricing. Brentwood dominates informational search rankings as a result. Publishing "starting at" ranges per linear foot with Good/Better/Best tiers, sample project costs, and a "What's Included" breakdown will capture comparison shoppers, pre-qualify leads, and differentiate Placed Right as the honest contractor in an opaque market.

**DO: Build dedicated service pages for each fence type AND city-specific landing pages for top 10 service areas** — The current single-page site cannot rank for any meaningful keyword. Individual pages for wood, vinyl, aluminum, chain link, pool fence, and fence repair — plus city pages for Manchester, Nashua, Bedford, Portsmouth, Dover, Hooksett, Goffstown, Hudson, Exeter, and Hampton — create the page architecture needed for local SEO dominance. Ultimate Fence's city page strategy proves this works.

**DO: Implement a response-time guarantee and communicate it everywhere** — The #1 complaint in NH fence reviews is getting ghosted. "We respond within 4 hours" backed by an automatic confirmation system (text/email) directly addresses the dominant buying blocker. This promise should appear on every page, in the estimate form confirmation, and in the GBP description.

**DO: Create an NH Fence Permit Guide and Pool Fence Code Compliance page as launch content** — These two informational pages face zero competition from fence contractors in search results. The permit guide covering 20+ NH towns and the pool code compliance page will rank quickly, establish authority, and generate high-intent leads from buyers in active planning stages.

### AVOID directives

**AVOID: Using a basic contact form with only Name/Email/Message fields** — The current 3-field form lacks a phone number field (critical in home services), provides no project context for the estimator, and creates cognitive burden by asking the customer to write an unstructured message. Research shows multi-step forms with visual selectors outperform basic forms by 2–3x in service categories. A structured multi-step form pre-qualifies leads and feels more professional.

**AVOID: Competing on years-in-business or scale metrics against established players** — Fences Unlimited has 55 years, Reliable has 70 years, and Superior has 30,000+ reviews. Placed Right cannot win a tenure or volume war. Instead, lean into what incumbents can't claim: personal attention from the owner on every project, genuine NH roots with specific frost-line expertise, and a brand personality that feels warm and human rather than corporate.

**AVOID: Launching without a Google Business Profile, review strategy, and at least 5 real testimonials** — The current site has zero social proof. In a category where ghosting is the #1 fear, a website with no reviews and no verifiable identity feels high-risk to buyers. At minimum, collect testimonials from early customers (even friends/family projects), claim and optimize the GBP, and create a systematic post-project review request process before driving any traffic to the site.

### EXPLOIT directives

**EXPLOIT: The Seacoast vacuum created by Platinum Fence's catastrophic website** — Platinum Fence dominates the Portsmouth/Hampton/Dover/Exeter corridor on reputation alone despite running a 2/10 website with no HTTPS, Flash placeholders, and a fax number. Any modern, mobile-optimized website with Seacoast-specific content and city landing pages would immediately compete for their digital traffic. Placed Right already lists Seacoast towns in their service area — building dedicated Seacoast content is the lowest-hanging competitive fruit in the market.

**EXPLOIT: The total absence of educational content about NH fence regulations, frost line engineering, and seasonal timing** — Zero NH fence contractors rank for "fence permit New Hampshire," "frost line depth New Hampshire fence," or "best time to install fence in NH." These informational searches represent buyers at the research stage who will convert 2–6 weeks later. By being the only fence company providing authoritative, NH-specific guidance on permits (town-by-town), frost line requirements (why posts must go 48+ inches deep), and seasonal timing (September–October is the ideal window), Placed Right captures these leads months before competitors see them.

**EXPLOIT: The emotional positioning gap where every competitor defaults to "professional/reliable/experienced" but none connect to the feeling of home** — The tagline "Love Wins Where Our Pickets Begin" is genuinely distinctive in a market of interchangeable "quality fencing solutions." Reviews prove buyers make emotional decisions — "massive stress reliever," "our pup loves that he can run free," "over the moon with my new fence." A website that leads with the emotional outcome (safety, freedom, peace of mind, pride in your home) rather than the product specifications (6-foot vinyl privacy panel, aluminum picket spacing) will convert at a higher rate than any competitor's feature-focused approach. The warm visual design (terracotta, serif typography, lifestyle photography) amplifies this positioning in a way no corporate navy-and-white template can match.
